Current research

My focus is road climate resilience in Cambridgeshire: predicting which segments of the local road network are most vulnerable to heavy rainfall events, and helping the council prioritise resilience investment under a fixed capital budget. The work combines regression analysis, tree ensembles for vulnerability scoring, and integer programming for the multi-year investment plan.

Recent projects

  • Rainfall–traffic regression on UK road sensor data, with HC3 robust standard errors
  • Random Forest comparison against logistic regression for predicting weather-related disruption
  • Mixed-integer programme for prioritising drainage and resurfacing upgrades under a council budget
  • PCA + clustering on Cambridgeshire road segments for vulnerability profiling
